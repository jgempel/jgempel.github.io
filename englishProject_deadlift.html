How to Perform a Deadlift



Introduction:


The deadlift acts as another essential powerlifting exercise for a beginner to learn and master. The exercise involves bending one’s knees, picking up an item, and standing straight up with the item in hand. The range of motion must be completed all while keeping one’s entire back straight.

Unlike bench press exercises, deadlifts focus on strengthening both the upper and lower body. This exercise’s main focus is on the glutes (or butt muscles) and lower back muscles. Deadlifts also help strengthen the quadriceps (or the front thigh muscles), hamstrings (or rear thigh muscles), adductors (or thigh muscles near the groin area), trapezius muscles (or muscles in the upper back), and forearm muscles [18]..


            Source: StrengthLog[19]


Materials:


NOTE: Machinery for specific lifts, such as the deadlift and squat, automatically give people enough space to perform those exercises. However, the deadlift does not use any machinery other than a barbell. Therefore, you are responsible for finding adequate space away from others.

  • Adequate space means that your deadlift location consists of an area of 4-6 feet in length by 8 feet in width[20]. This precaution will help maintain the safety of yourself and those around you.

The following materials required for the deadlift are labelled and described below. The numbers on the diagram correspond to each material needed, except for Number 4.

              Source: Valor Fitness[21]


  1. A barbell—a long, metallic rod that must be lifted by the exerciser.
  2. (Optional) Set of circular weights evenly distributed on the barbell.
  3. A sufficiently sized space to perform the deadlifts.
  4. Barbell clips to secure the circular weights. Refer to the Home Page's "Getting Started" section for instructions.
    • WARNING: Failure to use barbell clips can cause foot or other bodily injuries due to the circular weights falling off the barbell.
  5. A spotter—a person who stands beside you and ensures that you are safely performing the bench press. Highly recommended for beginning lifters.

DISCLAIMER: This web page only demonstrates a type of deadlift specifically performed with a barbell. Other variations of this exercise will not be covered here.


Step 1:Locate and Setup your Work Area


NOTE: Throughout Step 1, make sure the barbell is off the barbell bench and no weights are attached it.

  1. Find a sufficiently sized workout area in your gym.
    • As mentioned in the Materials section, make sure the area is at least 4-6 feet in length by 8 feet in width[20].
    • Make sure that other people can easily detour and walk away from your workout area.
    • If you are at a gym and struggle to find space, ask an employee for assistance on locating a sufficiently sized workout area.
    • WARNING: If your gym does not have a sufficiently sized workout area, do not proceed any further. Insufficient space to complete a deadlift can cause serious injury to yourself and others.
  2. Locate a barbell.
  3. Safely place the barbell in your workout area.
    • NOTE: A spotter is recommended for this step if you are an absolute beginner. If you have a spotter, lift one end of the barbell. Ask the spotter to lift the other end of the barbell. Carry the barbell to the workout area with the spotter’s help.
  4. Carefully place a barbell on the ground, one end before the other end.
  5. Insert any circular weights on the barbell if necessary. Make sure you use a barbell clip.
    • NOTE: More detailed instructions about installing circular weights on a barbell can be found on the Home page under the "Getting Started" section.

Step 2: Position your Body in the Proper Form


  1. Stand next to the barbell.
  2. Position your feet forward and shoulder-width apart, as close to the barbell as possible.
    1. If your barbell DOES have circular weights, place the toes of your feet underneath the barbell. Make sure your feet are parallel to the barbell.

      2.            Source: flickr[22]


    2. If your barbell DOES NOT have circular weight, place the toes of your feet adjacent to the barbell.
  3. Bend your knees until they form a 90-degree angle between your thighs and shins.
  4. Place your hands on the barbell shoulder-width apart with an overhand grip.

    5.              Source: BarBend.com[23]


  5. While in your current position, straighten your back and contract its muscles. Make sure that your spine and back do not bend throughout the exercise.
  6. While in your current position, move your buttocks (or glutes) outwards. Contract your glute muscles by pushing and flexing them.
  7. Ensure that your shoulders are positioned vertically above the barbell.

Step 3: Perform the Lift


WARNING: You must perform Step 3 in its entirety with your back straight and your spine in a neutral position. Failure to do so will result in serious back and neck injuries.

  1. Maintain your form, as explained in Step 2. Keep your back straight, then push your chest muscles outward.

    2.               Source: Everkinetic[24]


  2. Breathe inward slowly. Pull the barbell upwards until your body is perfectly straight.
    • NOTE: “Perfectly straight” means that you are in a standing position.
  3. After completing Step 3.2, your barbell should lie just below your stomach.

    4.               Source: Everkinetic[25]


  4. To begin lowering the barbell, breathe outward slowly, then gradually bend your hips. While following this sub-step, make sure your legs are almost perfectly straight.
  5. Continue bending your hips until the barbell lies below your knees.
  6. Afterwards, bend your legs to finish lowering the barbell onto the ground.
  7. Repeat Steps 3.1 through 3.6 four more times, or as recommended by a spotter or trainer.
  8. When you are finished with the exercise, place the barbell on the ground. Make sure you maintain proper form in this step.
